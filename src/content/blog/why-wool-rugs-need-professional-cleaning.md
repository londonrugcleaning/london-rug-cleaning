# Why Wool Rugs Need Professional Cleaning

Wool rugs are a popular choice for homeowners due to their durability, natural beauty, and soft texture. However, the unique structure of wool fibers means that they require specialized care to maintain their quality over time. While regular vacuuming is important, it's not enough to keep your wool rug truly clean. Hereâ€™s why professional cleaning is essential.

## 1. The Complexity of Wool Fibers

Wool is a natural fiber with a complex structure. Its outer layer has scales that can trap dirt, dust, and allergens deep within the rug pile. Standard vacuums often only remove surface debris, leaving the embedded soil to act like sandpaper, slowly wearing down the fibers every time you walk on the rug.

Professional cleaners use high-powered equipment and techniques like **dusting** (vibration) to dislodge this deep-seated soil before the washing process even begins.

## 2. Sensitivity to Water and Chemicals

Wool is highly absorbent and can hold significantly more than its weight in water. If not dried properly, a wool rug can develop mold, mildew, or a musty odor. Furthermore, wool is sensitive to high alkalinity. Many off-the-shelf carpet cleaners are too harsh (high pH) and can cause:
-   **Chemical burn** to the fibers.
-   **Color bleeding** (dye migration).
-   **Browning** or yellowing.

We use pH-balanced, wool-safe cleaning solutions that gently clean the fibers without stripping their natural oils (lanolin) or damaging the dyes.

## 3. Extending the Lifespan of Your Rug

A wool rug is an investment. With proper care, it can last for generations. Neglecting professional cleaning allows grit and grime to cut into the fiber base, causing premature wear and bald spots.

**Recommended Schedule:**
Most industry experts recommend professional cleaning every **12 to 18 months**, depending on foot traffic and whether you have pets.
